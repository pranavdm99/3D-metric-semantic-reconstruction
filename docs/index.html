3D Metric-Semantic Scene Reconstruction

An end-to-end computer vision pipeline converting raw RGB video and ARKit tracking into queryable mathematical 3D scene graphs via Semantic Loop Closure.

Introduction

Traditional Structure-from-Motion (SfM) architectures provide precise sparse tracking but lack the dense surface representation or semantic awareness required for complex spatial reasoning. This project integrates a unified, three-stage neural reconstruction pipeline that bridges this gap entirely from an RGB video, without relying on active LiDAR depth-sensors.

Our methodology simultaneously extracts raw Geometric Foundations (SfM Trajectory) and high-fidelity Neural Surfaces (3D Gaussian Splatting), which are then mathematically unified into a structured Topological Scene Graph. By intertwining open-vocabulary foundation models (YOLO World + SAM 2) with surface-aligned splatting (SuGaR), we achieve a grounded 3D representation where geometry and semantics are directly queryable through natural language.

Related Works

Our methodology builds upon the foundations of open-vocabulary 3D scene understanding, most notably drawing parallels to HOV-SG and ConceptGraphs. By lifting 2D foundation model outputs (YOLO World, SAM 2) into 3D space, we share the core objective of bridging low-level geometric reconstruction with high-level semantic reasoning. However, we implement several critical structural optimizations and physical grounding techniques to facilitate this specific use-case.

Surface Representation: While research baselines like ConceptGraphs primarily rely on discrete point or voxel representations, we utilize SuGaR (Surface-Aligned Splatting). This provides a continuous, mesh-like surface representation that is significantly higher fidelity and more efficient for downstream rendering and physics interactions.

Metric Grounding: A key feature of this implementation is the solution to the "dimensionless" nature of COLMAP. By integrating raw ARKit IMU trajectory data and applying an optimized Sim(3) transformation, we achieve a verified 1.7cm RMSE scale accuracy, providing true physical grounding from a single RGB video without relying on LiDAR or active depth sensors.

Semantic Loop Closure: We address the temporal tracking fragmentation common in SAM 2 through a dedicated Semantic Loop Closure algorithm. By autonomously merging fragmented point clouds based on both shared class-identity and tight 3D Euclidean proximity, we ensure a more consistent and robust Topological Scene Graph than methods relying solely on noisy feature-space similarities.

Methodology

The system architecture is designed as a modular, feed-forward pipeline that transitions from raw, unstructured RGB video into a structured mathematical 3D scene representation. As visualized in the flowchart to the right, the process begins by simultaneously extracting sparse camera trajectories through COLMAP (Phase 1) while performing zero-shot object detection and temporal tracking via YOLO World and SAM 2 (Phase 2). These parallel tracks are then unified through a Semantic Loop Closure algorithm that lifts 2D projections into a metric-aligned 3D SuGaR surface, finally yielding the queryable Topological Scene Graph.

Hardware & VRAM Engineering: Implementing this monolithic flow on consumer-grade hardware (Dell G15: 16 GB RAM, RTX 4060 8GB VRAM) required significant engineering pivots. We forcibly decoupled the geometric and semantic processing stages to prevent cumulative memory fragmentation during training. Furthermore, we developed runtime logic patches for the SuGaR CamerasWrapper modules, enabling dynamic resolution rescaling and hyper-parameter downscales to maintain high surface fidelity within a containerized, 8GB-constrained compute footprint.

Phase 1: 3D Surface Reconstruction

Testing on a 59-second video shot at 30 FPS, we decimated the temporal sequence down to 5 FPS (yielding ~295 working frames). The pipeline processes these frames through COLMAP Structure-from-Motion (SfM) to estimate initial sparse camera posings.

To achieve continuous physical geometry within constrained 8GB VRAM limits, we aggressively downscaled images globally (`factor=2`) and resolution (`factor=4`). After just 15 minutes of training, vanilla 3DGS peaked at approximately 1.4 million Gaussians. The SuGaR algorithm then rigorously optimized and pruned this surface down to a highly-refined, mesh-aligned cloud containing exactly 303,738 pristine 3D splats.

Phase 2: Semantic Loop Closure

Decoupled from 3D splatting and operating natively in 2D space, we utilize YOLO World and SAM 2 to perform zero-shot segmentation and temporal mask tracking across the heavily down-sampled image sequence.

Because temporal tracking masks frequently shatter across visual occlusions (e.g., separating a bed into bed_1 vs bed_2), we implement a mathematical Semantic Loop Closure technique. By lifting masks into the metric 3D cloud, we autonomously merge fragmented point clouds of identical string-classes if their 3D Euclidean bounding boxes fall within tight metric proximities.

Metric Scale Recovery

Because vanilla COLMAP reconstructions are strictly dimensionless logic puzzles, we extract the hardware iOS ARKit IMU trajectory natively associated with the video bounds. We rigidly align the two flight paths using an optimized Sim(3) transformation matrix.

$$ \mathbf{p}' = s \mathbf{R} \mathbf{p} + \mathbf{t} $$

Where \( \mathbf{p} \) represents the unscaled dimensionless coordinate, \( s \) is the isotropic scale factor expanding logic to metrics, \( \mathbf{R} \) is the proper \( 3 \times 3 \) rotational alignment, and \( \mathbf{t} \) is the \( 3 \times 1 \) translation vector mapping origin-to-origin yielding the absolute physical ARKit coordinate \( \mathbf{p}' \).

By enforcing this structural matrix over the entire point cloud, the scene forcefully snaps directly into real-world physical meters. This enabled our quantitative analysis to successfully identify a hyper-accurate, sub-2cm translation gap between the unscaled visual reconstruction path and the absolute ARKit sensor ground-truth—achieving a measured Root Mean Square Error (RMSE) of 0.0172 meters (1.72 cm) alongside a highly optimized 95th percentile (P95) deviation, confirming the camera trajectory remains rigorously grounded even against edge-case tracking outliers.

Topological Scene Graph

Once the scene is structurally snapped into true metric vectors, we compute physical Oriented Bounding Boxes (OBBs) for all discrete semantic entities extracted during Phase 2. By executing Principal Component Analysis (PCA) strictly along the gravity-aligned XZ floor plane, we cleanly eliminate any bounding-box skew distortions across tall geometries like doors and wardrobes.

These rigid arrays are evaluated against localized mathematical interaction algorithms to extract explicit geometry predicates (e.g., resting_on, directly_above, next_to)—outputting the formalized node-edge Topology Graph that ultimately bridges 3D mechanics to standard NLP querying.

Discussion

Sparse vs. Surface-Aligned Geometry

A careful audit of the raw structure-from-motion outputs reveals a fundamental limitation of traditional feature-tracking. The point distribution in COLMAP (SIFT) is extremely sparse and almost exclusively concentrated along high-contrast edges and texture boundaries. While this is SIFT performing exactly as designed, it leaves the central surfaces of objects (desks, monitors, doors) geometrically empty.

By contrast, SuGaR does not merely "densify" the cloud. It introduces critical surface regularization during the refinement stage. This ensures that Gaussians are mathematically pulled onto the actual manifold geometry of the scene, preventing the "floating splat" artifacts common in vanilla 3DGS and providing a continuous substrate for robust 3D semantic mask-lifting.

Spatial LLM Reasoning

Unlike traditional active VLM transformers that require massive 3D texturally colored splats and often hallucinate spatial scale, our methodology proved successful by systematically compiling the environment into an ultra-rigid Topological JSON Graph.

Through our intermediate layers, we successfully proved that a chaotic 1.4-million point cloud with unscaled visual noise could be actively refined to 300K primitives holding absolute 1.7cm (0.017m) tracking errors. When this geometrically grounded structure was passed natively to Ollama, the LLM successfully retrieved pure, scalable mathematical predicates capable of explicitly calculating Z-axis interactions without resorting to visual inference guesswork.

Conclusion

This study successfully demonstrates a holistic, end-to-end pipeline for converting unstructured RGB video into a metric-accurate, semantically-aware 3D scene representation. By rigidly aligning sparse COLMAP trajectories with absolute ARKit IMU data using an optimized Sim(3) transformation, we achieved a remarkable 1.7cm RMSE alignment accuracy on consumer-grade laptop hardware. The strategic decoupling of geometric reconstruction (SuGaR) and open-vocabulary tracking (YOLO World + SAM 2) enabled the generation of a queryable Topology Graph that yields grounded spatial predicates, effectively bypassing the visual hallucinations common in large vision-language models.

Impact and Applications: This rigid topological formulation directly abstracts physical reality into optimized predicates, which drastically accelerates several downstream use-cases:

  • Advanced Photogrammetry & Surveying: Leveraging the 1.72cm metric alignment error to perform real-world volume extraction without a LiDAR module.
  • Novel View Synthesis (NVS): Rapid architectural planning where designers can navigate camera orbits around cleanly identified, isolated sub-meshes instead of dense uniform arrays.
  • Spatial Q&A & Semantic Interaction: Enabling localized NLP-based spatial reasoning within complex physical environments, allowing for grounded semantic queries and interaction layers safely isolated from cloud latency.

Limitations: Currently, the pipeline's reliance on 2D temporal masks makes it susceptible to motion blur and non-rigid deformations during rapid camera movements. Furthermore, high-fidelity geometry extraction via surface-aligned splatting mathematically requires comprehensive orbital captures; linear translational trajectories inherently result in flattened occlusion planes along unobserved Z-axes.

Future Work: Future iterations will aim to unify semantic and geometric optimization by querying CLIP-embedding visual clustering vectors encoded natively into the 3D Gaussian Splats. This transition will eliminate the need for discrete 2D frame tracking and lift semantic understanding directly into the monolithic 3D optimization loop, further enhancing both speed and spatial coherence.
